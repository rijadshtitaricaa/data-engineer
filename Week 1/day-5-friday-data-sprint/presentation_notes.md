## 1. What dataset did we analyze?
We analyzed an orders dataset containing customer names, cities, products, categories, quantities, prices, order status, and order dates.

## 2. What did we do in Python?
We used Python to read the CSV file, process the order data, calculate total amounts, find completed revenue, count orders, and generate a business report.

## 3. What did we do in SQL?
We created an orders table, inserted the data, filtered orders by status, calculated revenue, grouped orders by city and category, and found the most valuable orders.

## 4. What was our most important business insight?
Our most important insight was that completed sales generated €1601.50 in revenue and Electronics was the strongest category with the highest number of orders.

## 5. What was difficult?
The most difficult part was making sure the Python calculations and SQL queries produced the same results and understanding how to analyze data from a business perspective.

## 6. What would we improve if we had more time?
We would create more visual reports, analyze trends over time, and add more business metrics.
